Audio samples from "Timbre Disentanglement Exploration in Non-Autoregressive Cross-Lingual Text-to-Speech"

Abstract: How to make better use of the controllability of the non-autoregressive model to improve the cross-lingual Text-to-Speech(TTS) model is a problem worthy of research. In this paper, we propose a FastPitch-based cross-Lingual TTS model with language-dependent representation. We evaluate the effect of using different numbers of training speakers in FastPitch-based multilingual model and explore the controllability of FastPitch in Mandarin-dominated cross-lingual utterances. Our experimental results show that the proposed approach could achieve decent speaker consistency and similarity with multi-speaker datasets and explicit pitch information modeling, improve the naturalness of Mandarin-dominated mixed-lingual utterances by utilizing the controllability of non-autoregressive model and our proposed phoneme length regulator.
